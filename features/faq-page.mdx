## Overview

The FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) page helps guests find answers to common questions about your wedding without having to contact you directly. This saves time for both you and your guests while ensuring everyone has the information they need.

## Page Structure

The `pages/faqs.html` file provides a clean question-and-answer format with navigation links:

```html faqs.html
<article id="main">
    <header>
        <h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
        <p>Some extra info for folks</p>
    </header>
    <section class="wrapper style5">
        <div class="inner">
            <section id="Confirm Attendance">
                <h2>What's the weather like?</h2>
                <p>
                    <blockquote>
                        <b>Hot with a good chance of rain.</b><br />
                        <i>Average Highs of 91.4°F / 33°C</i><br />
                        <i>Average Lows of 71.6°F / 22°C</i><br />
                        All in all, it's pretty darn hot in May, so channel 
                        your chi by bringing airy, tropics-friendly clothes.
                    </blockquote>
                </p>
                <!-- More questions follow -->
            </section>
        </div>
    </section>
</article>
```

## Best Practices for FAQ Content

### Use Blockquotes for Answers

The template uses `<blockquote>` tags to visually distinguish answers from questions:

```html
<h2>Question goes here?</h2>
<p>
    <blockquote>
        Answer goes here with all relevant details.
    </blockquote>
</p>
```

This creates visual hierarchy and makes the page scannable.

### Include Internal Links

Link to other pages on your site where guests can find more information:

```html
<a href="schedule.html">Click here to take you to the schedule page</a>
```

### Provide Multiple Contact Options

```html
<a href="#footer">Click here to contact us via social media, email or a quick call</a>
```

The `#footer` anchor link jumps to the footer where all contact information is displayed.

### Be Specific with Details

Instead of vague answers, provide concrete information:

- **Vague**: "The venue has parking"
- **Specific**: "Parking for 300 cars on first-come, first-served basis plus street parking"

## Creating Your FAQ Page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Common Questions">
    Think about questions you've already received or anticipate:
    - Review your event details for potential confusion points
    - Ask friends who've recently married what they were asked
    - Consider your specific venue's quirks or policies
    - Think about your guest list's needs (elderly, children, out-of-towners)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Organize by Category">
    Group related questions together:
    
    ```html
    <section>
        <h2>Logistics</h2>
        <h3>How do I get to the venue?</h3>
        <!-- Answer -->
        
        <h3>Where should guests park?</h3>
        <!-- Answer -->
    </section>
    
    <section>
        <h2>What to Expect</h2>
        <h3>What time should I arrive?</h3>
        <!-- Answer -->
        
        <h3>How long is the ceremony?</h3>
        <!-- Answer -->
    </section>
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write Clear, Complete Answers">
    Each answer should:
    - Directly address the question
    - Provide specific details (numbers, times, locations)
    - Link to relevant pages for more information
    - Offer alternative options when applicable
    
    <Tip>
      Write answers as if you're explaining to someone who's never been to a wedding 
      like yours before. Don't assume cultural or local knowledge.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Cross-Links">
    Link to other pages where guests can find related information:
    
    ```html
    For more details, see our 
    <a href="schedule.html">schedule page</a> or 
    <a href="travel_and_accommodation.html">travel information</a>.
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

```html

        we have registries at [Store Name]. Links are available on our 
        <a href="registry.html">registry page</a>.
    </blockquote>
```

### Photography Policy
```html
<h2>Can I take photos during the ceremony?</h2>
<p>
    <blockquote>
        We ask that guests refrain from taking photos during the ceremony 
        so our professional photographer can capture the best moments. 
        Please feel free to take photos during the reception!
    </blockquote>
</p>
```

### Plus-One Policy
```html
<h2>Can I bring a date?</h2>
<p>
    <blockquote>
        Due to space limitations, we're only able to accommodate guests 
        specifically named on your invitation. If your invitation says 
        "and guest," you're welcome to bring a plus-one!
    </blockquote>
</p>
```

### Dietary Restrictions
```html
<h2>I have dietary restrictions. Will there be options for me?</h2>

    <blockquote>

        with our caterer to accommodate vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, 
        and allergy needs.
    </blockquote>
</p>
```

## Updating FAQs

<Warning>
  Keep your FAQ page updated as you:
  - Receive new questions from guests
  - Make changes to wedding plans
  - Add or modify venue policies
  - Update COVID or health protocols
</Warning>

Set a reminder to review and update your FAQ:
- When you send invitations
- One month before the wedding
- One week before the wedding (for last-minute details)

## Analytics and Tracking

After your wedding, review which questions guests asked most:

- Check Google Forms responses for patterns
- Note which questions came up in conversations
- Use website analytics to see which FAQ answers were most viewed

This helps you refine the FAQ for maximum usefulness.

<Tip>
  The questions guests ask most frequently should be moved to the top of your FAQ 
  or even highlighted on your homepage.
</Tip>
